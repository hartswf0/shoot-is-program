Flusser-Model — 4 Platforms in Entities & Morphisms

Unpacking the Code of Photography and Reality

Welcome to Flusser Model, four platforms defined by entities and morphisms. Unpacking the code of photography and reality.

1) <Apparatus> as <Super-Black-Box>

Reality is produced by nested programs; humans operate as functionaries at the edges.

Platform one: The Apparatus as a Super Black Box. Reality is produced by nested programs; humans operate as functionaries at the edges.

Entities of the Apparatus

  • <Apparatus>: a programmed <black-box>.
  • <Program>: finite but vast <rule-set>.
  • <Metaprogram>: higher-order <rule-set> that constrains <program>.
  • <Functionary>: human operator at I/O.
  • <Output-World>: perceived “reality” generated by apparatuses.

Entities of the Apparatus include: The Apparatus itself, a programmed black box. The Program, a finite but vast rule set. The Metaprogram, a higher-order rule set that constrains the program. The Functionary, a human operator at input/output. And the Output World, our perceived reality generated by these apparatuses.

Morphisms of the Apparatus

  • [generates]: <Apparatus> × <Program> → <Output-World>
  • [constrains]: <Metaprogram> → <Program>
  • [operates]: <Functionary> → <Apparatus>
  • [hides]: <Black-Box> → <Internal-State>
  • [shifts-power-to]: <Software> → <Programmer>

Morphisms define their interactions: The Apparatus, combined with its Program, generates the Output World. A Metaprogram constrains the Program. A Functionary operates the Apparatus. The Black Box hides its Internal State. And power shifts to the Programmer through the Software.

Sitemap: Super-Black-Box Structure

<Super-Black-Box>
├── <Metaprogram> [constrains] <Program>
├── <Apparatus> [generates] <Output-World>
│   └── <Black-Box> [hides] <Internal-State>
└── <Functionary> [operates] <Apparatus>

The Super Black Box reveals its nested structure: Metaprograms constrain Programs. The Apparatus generates our Output World, with its Black Box hiding its Internal State. And the Functionary operates the Apparatus.

2) <Technical-Image> and the “Magic of Objectivity”

Photos look objective but actually encode theories and programs.

Platform two: The Technical Image and the Magic of Objectivity. Photos look objective but actually encode theories and programs.

Entities of the Technical Image

  • <Technical-Image>: third-order <abstraction> (texts → programs → images).
  • <Concepts/Text>: scientific/theoretical inputs.
  • <Surface>: medium that carries the image.
  • <Viewer>: decoder/recipient.

Entities of the Technical Image: The Technical Image itself, a third-order abstraction. Concepts or Text, which are scientific or theoretical inputs. The Surface, the medium carrying the image. And the Viewer, who is the decoder or recipient.

Morphisms of the Technical Image

  • [encodes]: <Concepts/Text> → <Technical-Image>
  • [renders-as]: <Program> → <Surface>
  • [appears-as-objective]: <Technical-Image> → <Viewer>
  • [must-be-decoded]: <Viewer> → <Concepts/Text>

Morphisms for the Technical Image: Concepts or Text encode the Technical Image. A Program renders to a Surface. The Technical Image appears as objective to the Viewer. And critically, the Viewer must decode the image back to Concepts or Text.

Sitemap: Image as Abstraction

<Technical-Image>
├── <Concepts/Text> [encodes] → <Image-Program> [renders-as] → <Surface>
└── <Viewer>
    ├── (naïve) [appears-as-objective]
    └── (critical) [must-be-decoded] → <Concepts/Text>

The Technical Image, a complex abstraction: Concepts encode an Image Program, which renders to a Surface. A naive Viewer sees it as objective, but a critical Viewer must decode it to the underlying Concepts.

3) <Freedom> as [Playing-Against-The-Program]

Human freedom = creating improbable, informative moves inside (and against) the program.

Platform three: Freedom as Playing Against the Program. Human freedom means creating improbable, informative moves inside and against the program.

Entities of Freedom

  • <Photographer>: <player> not worker.
  • <Camera-Program>: finite move-set.
  • <Informative-Image>: improbable output (low redundancy).
  • <Quantum-Doubt>: micro-hesitations/decisions.
  • <Metaprogram-Design>: redesign of the rules themselves.

Entities of Freedom: The Photographer, who is a player not a worker. The Camera Program, a finite move set. An Informative Image, an improbable output with low redundancy. Quantum Doubt, which are micro-hesitations and decisions. And Metaprogram Design, the redesign of the rules themselves.

Morphisms of Freedom

  • [plays-against]: <Photographer> → <Camera-Program>
  • [produces]: <Play> → <Informative-Image>
  • [navigates-via]: <Photographer> → <Quantum-Doubt>
  • [smuggles-intent-into]: <Photographer> → <Program>
  • [escalates-to]: <Play> → <Metaprogram-Design>

Morphisms for Freedom: The Photographer plays against the Camera Program. Play produces an Informative Image. The Photographer navigates via Quantum Doubt. The Photographer smuggles intent into the Program. And Play escalates to Metaprogram Design.

Sitemap: Freedom as Play

<Freedom>
├── <Photographer> [plays-against] <Camera-Program>
│   └── [navigates-via] <Quantum-Doubt>
├── <Play> [produces] <Informative-Image>
└── <Play> [escalates-to] <Metaprogram-Design>

Freedom emerges as a dynamic interplay: The Photographer plays against the Camera Program, navigating via Quantum Doubt. This Play produces Informative Images and can escalate to Metaprogram Design.

4) <Distribution-Channel> as Encoder of Significance

Meaning is assigned downstream; channels program reception.

Platform four: The Distribution Channel as Encoder of Significance. Meaning is assigned downstream; channels program reception.

Entities of Distribution

  • <Distribution-Channel>: scientific / political / commercial / artistic pipelines.
  • <Photograph>: portable <carrier> of potential meanings.
  • <Encoded-Significance>: role the image takes in a channel.
  • <Audience>: targeted receivers.

Entities of Distribution: The Distribution Channel, which are pipelines like scientific or artistic. The Photograph, a portable carrier of potential meanings. The Encoded Significance, the role the image takes in a channel. And the Audience, targeted receivers.

Morphisms of Distribution

  • [re-signifies]: <Distribution-Channel> × <Photograph> → <Encoded-Significance>
  • [anticipates]: <Photographer> → <Distribution-Channel>
  • [programs]: <Channel> → <Audience-Perception>
  • [conflicts-with]: <Photographer-Intention> ↔ <Channel-Program>

Morphisms for Distribution: The Distribution Channel, with the Photograph, re-signifies into Encoded Significance. The Photographer anticipates the Distribution Channel. The Channel programs Audience Perception. And Photographer Intention often conflicts with the Channel Program.

Sitemap: Distribution Field

<Distribution-Field>
├── <Photograph>
│   └── <Channel₁…ₙ> [re-signifies] → <Encoded-Significance₁…ₙ>
└── <Audience> ⇐ [programs] <Channel>

The Distribution Field, a network of signification: A Photograph flows through various Channels, each re-signifying it into specific Encoded Significance. This Channel then programs the Audience's perception.
