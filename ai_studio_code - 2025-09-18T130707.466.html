Spencer-Brown Inspired Formal Models

Jurgenson and Flusser's Arguments

Structured Index of Theoretical Components

Jurgenson's Anti-Dualist Framework

Primary Distinction Structure:

  • ◻₁ Digital Dualism → The false belief that online and offline experiences constitute separate, hierarchically ordered realms
  • ◻₂ Augmented Reality → The unmarked/integrated state representing lived reality at the intersection of digital and physical
  • ◻₃ Online/Offline Binary → Sub-distinction creating false separation between connected and disconnected states
  • ◻₄ IRL Fetish → Pathological fixation valorizing disconnected "real life" experience
  • ◻₅ Social Photo → Transformed photographic practice as communication rather than documentation

Jurgenson's Anti-Dualist Framework

Morphological Operations:

  • →₁ Dualistic Critique: ◻₁ ⊢ ¬◻₁ → ◻₂ (Critical dissolution of false binary revealing integrated reality)
  • →₂ Logical Transformation: (A ∨ ¬A) → (A ∧ ¬A) (Movement from exclusive to inclusive logic)
  • →₃ Therapeutic Dissolution: ◻₄ → ¬◻₄ → ◻₂ (Resolution of disconnection anxiety)
  • →₄ Media Transformation: Documentary photo → Social communication medium

Flusser's Apparatus Theory

Primary Distinction Structure:

  • ■₁ Apparatus → Technological system containing embedded programs that constrain possible outputs
  • ■₂ Human Gesture → Creative intervention attempting to insert unprogrammed information
  • ■₃ Program → Pre-inscribed possibilities that appear as freedom while constraining choice
  • ■₄ Freedom → Capacity to "outwit" programmed constraints through skilled manipulation
  • ■₅ Technical Image → Third-order abstraction: image of concepts rather than world
  • ■₆ Post-History → Cybernetic condition where feedback loops replace linear causality

Flusser's Apparatus Theory

Morphological Operations:

  • ⟶₁ Gestural Intervention: ■₁ ⊢ ■₂ → (■₁ ∧ ■₂) (Apparatus operation becomes creative through human skill)
  • ⟶₂ Program Resistance: ■₃ → ¬■₃ → ■₄ (Photographer moves from following to outwitting program)
  • ⟶₃ Historical Progression: Magic → History → PostHistory (Evolutionary media epochs)
  • ⟶₄ Feedback Loop: ■₁ ⟷ ■₂ → ■₁∪■₂ (Dialectical apparatus-operator relationship)

Categorical Structure (OLOG Framework)

Objects:

  • E (Experience) - Lived reality as hybrid formation
  • T (Technology) - Technical systems and apparatus
  • H (Human Agency) - Creative intervention and resistance
  • S (Social Practice) - Meaningful cultural activities

Morphisms:

  • mediated_by: E → T (Experience is technologically mediated)
  • enables: T → H (Technology enables/constrains agency)
  • creates: H → S (Agency produces meaningful practices)
  • shapes: S → E (Practices recursively constitute experience)

Commutative Diagram:

E → T → H → S → E

(Circular co-determination)

Convergent Logical Structures

Both theorists develop anti-binary critiques:

  • Jurgenson: ¬(Online ∨ Offline) → AugmentedReality
  • Flusser: ¬(Human ∨ Machine) → ApparatusComplex

Convergent Logical Structures (cont.)

Both propose dialectical resolutions:

  • Jurgenson: Digital ∧ Physical = IntegratedExperience
  • Flusser: Apparatus ∧ Gesture = TechnicalCreativity

Both analyze constrained agency:

Shared Theorem: ∀x ∈ TechnicalPractice: Programmed(x) ∧ Creative(x)

All technical practices are simultaneously programmed by systems AND creative through human intervention.

Meta-Theoretical Implications

The Spencer-Brown formalization reveals shared logical structures:

  • 1. Identification of false binaries that appear natural but are actually constructed
  • 2. Critical dissolution of these binaries through theoretical analysis
  • 3. Proposal of synthetic categories that capture the actual complexity of socio-technical relationships
  • 4. Maintenance of space for human agency within systemic constraints

Post-Binary Media Theory

Both theorists contribute to approaches that move beyond simple human/technology, natural/artificial, or online/offline distinctions toward more sophisticated dialectical analyses.

The fundamental insight both share is that:

all experience in technological societies is hybrid

— neither purely human nor purely technical, but constituted through ongoing processes of human-technology entanglement.
