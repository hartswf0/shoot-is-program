Jurgenson and Flusser's Arguments

A Media Studies Perspective

Jurgenson's Anti-Dualist Framework

Key Concepts:

  • The false belief that online and offline are separate worlds.◻₁ Digital Dualism
  • Our integrated, lived reality where digital and physical seamlessly merge.◻₂ Augmented Reality
  • The mistaken idea that online and offline are distinct, unconnected states.◻₃ Online/Offline Binary
  • An unhealthy obsession with "real life" as superior to digitally-mediated experience.◻₄ IRL Fetish
  • Photography evolved into a social communication tool, not just for documentation.◻₅ Social Photo

Jurgenson's Anti-Dualist Framework

How Concepts Transform:

  • Critical Dissolution: By challenging Digital Dualism, we reveal integrated Augmented Reality. →₁ Dualistic Critique: ◻₁ ⊢ ¬◻₁ → ◻₂
  • Logical Shift: Moving from "either/or" thinking to "both/and" thinking about digital and physical. →₂ Exclusive to Inclusive Logic: (A ∨ ¬A) → (A ∧ ¬A)
  • Therapeutic Resolution: Overcoming the IRL Fetish allows us to embrace Augmented Reality. →₃ Dissolving Disconnection: ◻₄ → ¬◻₄ → ◻₂
  • Media Evolution: Photography transforms from mere documentation to a medium for social communication. →₄ Photo's New Role: Documentary photo → Social communication medium

Flusser's Apparatus Theory

Key Concepts:

  • A technological system (e.g., camera) with embedded programs that limit its outputs.■₁ Apparatus
  • A human's creative, unprogrammed interaction with the apparatus.■₂ Human Gesture
  • The pre-set rules or possibilities within an apparatus that *appear* to offer freedom but are constrained.■₃ Program
  • The ability to "outwit" or creatively manipulate the apparatus's programmed limits.■₄ Freedom
  • Images that represent abstract concepts rather than directly showing the world.■₅ Technical Image
  • A cybernetic condition where constant feedback loops replace linear historical progression.■₆ Post-History

Flusser's Apparatus Theory

How Concepts Transform:

  • Creative Intervention: Human gesture transforms the apparatus, making its operation creative. ⟶₁ Gestural Intervention: ■₁ ⊢ ■₂ → (■₁ ∧ ■₂)
  • Program Resistance: The photographer moves from following the program to creatively outwitting it. ⟶₂ Outwitting the Program: ■₃ → ¬■₃ → ■₄
  • Media Epochs: Tracing the evolution of communication from mythic to linear to cybernetic. ⟶₃ Historical Progression: Magic → History → PostHistory
  • Dynamic Interaction: A continuous, back-and-forth feedback loop between the apparatus and the operator. ⟶₄ Apparatus-Operator Feedback: ■₁ ⟷ ■₂ → ■₁∪■₂

Categorical Structure (OLOG Framework)

Key Elements (Objects):

  • E (Experience): Our lived reality as a blend of digital and physical.
  • T (Technology): The technical systems and tools we use.
  • H (Human Agency): Our creative choices and resistance within systems.
  • S (Social Practice): Meaningful cultural activities and interactions.

Connecting Relationships (Morphisms):

  • Experience is mediated by Technology: E → T
  • Technology enables (and constrains) Human Agency: T → H
  • Human Agency creates Social Practices: H → S
  • Social Practices shape our Experience: S → E

Circular Co-determination:

E → T → H → S → E

This diagram illustrates the constant, reciprocal influence among these elements in our techno-social world.

Convergent Logical Structures

Both theorists reject "either/or" thinking:

  • Jurgenson: It's not "online OR offline." Our reality is always integrated Augmented Reality. ¬(Online ∨ Offline) → AugmentedReality
  • Flusser: It's not "human OR machine." We exist in an inseparable Apparatus Complex. ¬(Human ∨ Machine) → ApparatusComplex

Convergent Logical Structures (cont.)

Both propose new syntheses:

  • Jurgenson: The Digital AND the Physical combine to form an Integrated Experience. Digital ∧ Physical = IntegratedExperience
  • Flusser: The Apparatus AND Human Gesture result in Technical Creativity. Apparatus ∧ Gesture = TechnicalCreativity

Both recognize agency within systems:

Shared Theorem: Every technical practice is simultaneously Programmed AND Creative.

Our engagement with technology always involves both following its rules and finding ways to express ourselves creatively within (or against) them.

Meta-Theoretical Implications

This formal modeling reveals shared patterns in Jurgenson and Flusser's theories:

  • 1. Identifying False Binaries: They expose distinctions that seem natural but are constructed.
  • 2. Dissolving Binaries: They break down these false separations through critical analysis.
  • 3. Proposing New Categories: They offer complex, integrated concepts for socio-technical relationships.
  • 4. Preserving Human Agency: They emphasize human creativity and resistance even within systemic constraints.

Post-Binary Media Theory

Both theorists lay foundations for understanding media beyond simple "human vs. technology" or "online vs. offline" divides.

Their fundamental shared insight:

All experience in technological societies is hybrid.

It's never purely human nor purely technical, but a continuous interplay of human-technology entanglement.
